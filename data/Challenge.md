# Case Development Framework

## 1. Topic of the Case
**Health Care Data Harmonization / Data Mapping**

## 2. Case Title
**Smart Health Data Mapping: From Raw Data to Insights**

## 3. Case Summary

Healthcare data originates from multiple heterogeneous systems, including hospital information systems, laboratory software, Excel exports, and even free-text reports. These sources vary widely in format, semantics, and data quality. Current harmonization and processing require numerous manual steps, leading to inefficiency and limited scalability.

The objective is to develop an intelligent application that automates data mapping across all formats and sources. Whether dealing with structured tables, PDFs, or unstructured text, the solution should autonomously identify, align, and transform data into a unified target model.

The application should include an interactive dashboard displaying:

- Data origin and structure
- Data quality and completeness
- Detected anomalies or inconsistencies
- Alerts for quality or mapping issues
- Manual correction interface for mapping errors

### Primary Users
Internal teams across product development, data analytics, and strategic units, who will gain an integrated, interoperable, and high-quality data foundation for analysis and innovation.

## 4. Data

Participants will receive anonymized real-world healthcare data complemented by synthetic datasets where needed. These include:

- Care assessments and scores (e.g. mobility, fall risk, nutrition)
- Care interventions
- Medical data (diagnoses, procedures)
- Laboratory parameters
- Sensor data
- Vital signs
- Medication plans
- Staff scheduling and resource planning data

## 5. Technology

- Expected use of modern technologies such as AI-driven pattern recognition, NLP for free text, autonomous data agents, and rule-based or learning-based mapping logic
- No fixed technology stack; SQL database integration (compatible with **epaSOLUTIONS**) is preferred
- The solution should be deployable on-premises ("offline capable") to ensure data remains securely in-house

## 6. Use Case and Business Relevance

Hospitals and long-term care institutions generate large volumes of routine data daily, from structured assessments to unstructured documentation. However, much of this information is underused for predictive or operational insights.

Building on data from around **1,400 institutions** and **630,000 active users**, the aim is to create a digital twin of care situations through systematic data harmonization. The resulting unified dataset enables:

- Enhanced internal analytics, model training, and product development
- Improved comparability and scalability of solutions
- Greater innovation speed and efficiency through a consolidated data architecture

### Market Potential
The DACH healthcare market generates roughly **€100 billion** in annual costs (**€80 billion in Germany alone**). Even a modest **1–2% efficiency gain** represents **€800 million to €1.6 billion** in savings.

### Example Calculation
- 5% of inpatient cases incur avoidable complications
- Additional cost per case: **€3,000–5,000**
- Predictive prevention could reduce these by **10–20%**, yielding savings in the hundreds of millions annually
